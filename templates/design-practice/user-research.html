Our research practice

User research gives us access to our users' thoughts, feelings, and actions. We use quantitative methods to address the "how much" and "when" type of questions, while qualitative approaches explore the "how" and "why" of our products. Below are some of the techniques we frequently use.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/b04abfac-user-interviews.png", alt="", width="852", height="499", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
User interviews

To explore with users their entire workflow, goals, attitudes and decision-making  during generative phases of research.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/33b2ea56-treetests.png", alt="", width="852", height="499", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
Tree tests

To understand their mental model and how they name things, to match their view to how we organise information in our products.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/030feabe-micro-surveys.png", alt="", width="852", height="499", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
Micro surveys

To get a large sample on specific questions through short, rapid surveys aim at a low-friction engagement to evaluate our work.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/46ff73eb-heatmaps.png", alt="", width="852", height="499", hi_def=True, attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"}, loading="lazy") | safe }}
Heatmaps

To get behavioural data on how people are interacting with a page, and identify dark areas of content that's not being engaged with.

Our commitment to ethical research

What is ethical research?

Ethical research respects participants' rights, ensuring their consent, privacy, and well-being. It involves transparent practices and avoids harm, prioritising honesty and integrity in all interactions.

Why ethical research is important

Sticking to ethical standards builds trust, ensures data accuracy, and protects participants from harm. It preserves credibility and reliability, creating a positive impact on both the research and its outcomes.

Embedding ethics into processes

We follow strict guidelines to respect privacy, avoid unnecessary data collection, and adhere to GDPR and international law requirements. Our practices include obtaining clear consent, safeguarding data, and prioritising transparency throughout the research process.

Diverse perspectives to build the right products the right way

Our dedicated team of user researchers focuses on conducting strategic and discovery studies for our key products, ensuring a deep understanding of user needs within the tech landscape. UX designers are encouraged to lead evaluative studies, such as usability tests and card sorting exercises. Researchers guide them through the entire process, from planning to data analysis.

Things we do

Our product portfolio is constantly evolving, and we are determined to keep our vision centred around our users’ needs. We conduct discovery research to guide the direction of product development, and iterative research to fine-tune design and engineering progress.

These case studies show how our approach drives advancements and defines the future of our products.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/1e534edf-charmhub-redesign.png", alt="", width="852", height="1279", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
CharmHub redesign

We conducted interviews to explore users’ mental model of how charm interfaces work. Using co-design activities we identified user information needs, developed the information architecture, and determined navigation preferences for the new interface pages.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/cd9e03a3-new-support-portal.png", alt="", width="852", height="1279", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
Launch of the new support portal

We conducted usability tests and observational studies to evaluate the UX and identify unmet needs of the new support portal. We examined how customers manage tickets, how our support integrates with their practices, including searching for answers in existing documentation.

{{ image(url="https://assets.ubuntu.com/v1/353145aa-cves.png", alt="", width="852", height="1279", hi_def=True, loading="lazy", attrs={"class": "p-image-container__image"},) | safe }}
CVEs pages update

We investigated how users search Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs), what information they seek, and how they use it. Using interviews, heatmaps, search query analysis, and prototype testing, we developed a new information design, navigation system, and filter options.
