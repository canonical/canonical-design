Designing in the open

We bring the spirit of Ubuntu to life

We embody the philosophy of Ubuntu to lead open source and design towards a more collaborative future. We work in the open, base our decisions on research, and make sure the experiences we intend for our users are accessible and available.

Our design principles

Human by design

Our mission is to bring free software to the widest audience. As designers, we make sure that software is accessible to everyone.

We listen to our users and take user research seriously. We engage with our community, and align our work with the principle of humanity to others, creating solutions that are meaningful and impactful.

How we do user research ›

Accessible to everyone

Open source is at our core. But just because anyone can get our code, doesn’t mean it’s accessible. We make sure that every user has a great experience with our products, regardless of their abilities.

This also means our designs go beyond the usual graphic user interfaces. We also consider command line functionality, inclusive language, and how to write documentation.

Our accessibility practice ›

Inherently complex

From Ubuntu to enterprise solutions, our diverse portfolio spans simple to complex products. Practised users demand sophisticated features, challenging us to balance depth with user-friendliness.

Despite varying complexity, we strive to create a cohesive ecosystem. Our goal is a seamless experience across all products.

Designed in the open

We are open, transparent and collaborative throughout our design process. We share our work, seek feedback and engage with our community to improve quality and build trust.

It’s how we align with Canonical’s core values of open source and community collaboration.

Beautifully precise

We emphasise precision. Attention to detail, clarity and consistency shape every aspect of our design.

We embrace our brand guidelines so users have a cohesive and visually appealing experience across all products and platforms.

How we work

Own the work,
rely on the team

Our team is fully remote. Since we work apart, designers are each fully responsible for the end-to-end experience of their product. That doesn’t mean we’re alone.

We rely on peer reviews and good communication to keep design a team effort and align our products. We each have a lot of responsibility, and enough support to carry it out.

Collaborate across functions

We work in cross-functional teams of engineers, product managers, data analysts and technical authors. Together we ensure that every facet of our products is well aligned.

We bridge the gap between ideas and implementation with diagrams and models to visualise complex technical architecture and abstract concepts. This helps us communicate and solve problems quickly.

Grow together

We are committed to improving our design practice. We have three working groups, with each designer dedicating 20% of their time to one of them. Each working group plays a crucial role in advancing our design practices at Canonical and beyond.
