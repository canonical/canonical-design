How design contributions can help your project

Opening your project to design contributions increase your chances to create something great, but also to build a more inclusive and engaged community.

Improve usability and accessibility

Benefit from user-centric design that improves usability and accessibility.

Include more diverse perspectives

Gain fresh, innovative ideas from designers with different backgrounds and expertise.

Create a recognisable identity

Consistent, high-quality design helps establish and maintain a strong visual identity.

Broaden your reach

Attract a broader range of contributors, boosting project visibility and support.

Create better documentation

Create more intuitive and beginner-friendly documentation.

Stay ahead of the curve

Stay ahead by continuously integrating design trends and best practices into your project.

Attract the best designers
for your project

One of the biggest hurdles faced by designers interested in contributing is the lack of clear guidelines on how to contribute and what is expected of them. Here are some things you can do to help designers start contributing to your project.

Be open and clear

  • Create a brief for your project and your request. Explain what you would like to receive, why and how and outline the skills required.

Set up expectations

  • Make sure the acceptance criteria are clear
  • Explicitly state if the job is paid or free
  • Explain what license you are using in easy-to-understand terms
  • Be transparent about your design review process

Be responsive

  • Try to stay in touch and provide feedback quickly
  • Make sure to be constructive and refer to the acceptance criteria for approval/rejection of a contribution
