Impactful design at scale

Striking a balance between technical complexity and usable interfaces

Our design systems include documentation, reusable components, and patterns to speed up design and development, letting us focus on bigger challenges. We develop new components with all our products in mind, ensuring they fit our range of use cases.

Sharing ownership for all design system users

Managing a design system that is owned by several teams is a challenge.

This is why we created a hierarchy of subsystems that assign ownership to their relevant teams. We understand that the flow of new ideas starts at the individual product level and makes its way up to higher parts of the systemâ€™s hierarchy. We also apply our brand values from the top down, so that the lower levels of the hierarchy remain consistent.

Defined, documented, measured

Our approach to delivering design quality

Defined quality standards

We want our design system to set the standard for open source design. To do this, we set clear rules for what quality means. We then use these rules to check our designs, code, and documentation and make sure that our design system maintains high standards.

Design and code documentation

We check how complete our documentation is to see if it's correct. We share the guidelines with users even if they're not perfect, helping users save time and focus on what matters. This practical approach helps us cover more ground and improve our documentation over time.

Measuring our output

Metrics help us understand how the design system is being used in our team. We check how often our teams use standard parts versus making their own. It helps us see if our standard parts need improvement. We also keep track of how much each team adds to our system. This keeps everyone involved and helps us spread the work across more teams.

Accessibility

We care about making our components follow accessibility rules, to facilitate complying with standards in our products. Read more about how we tackle accessibility at Canonical.

About the design system team

Our Design System team consists of UX designers, UX writers, and Visual designers, who spend 20% of their time ensuring consistency and quality across our design system. They're supported by three web engineers who focus on Vanilla. For Ubuntu and its desktop apps, we team up with Flutter developers and the community.

Read more about our design efforts and working groups on the design practice page.
